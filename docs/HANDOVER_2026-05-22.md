## Executive summary (2026-05-25 — arc conclusion)

We have run **8 experiments (E1–E8)** on a BANKNIFTY ATM weekly long-option strategy with ML entry timing (AUC ~0.65). After 5+ months of research, **no configuration has survived OOS validation.**

| Layer | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| Entry timing | AUC ~0.65 — real edge, but not enough alone (PF ~1.0) |
| Direction (CE/PE) | AUC ceiling 0.54–0.557 across 3 training attempts — **decisive null** |
| CE-only filter | CE leg PF 1.36–1.42 vs PE 0.79–0.89 — structural asymmetry, but not enough |
| Time-window filter | Top-3 windows PF 1.72–1.85 in-sample; **collapsed OOS** |
| Regime filter | NOT-bull combined_majority, in-sample PF 3.00; **collapsed OOS** (PF 0.54) |
| Bar-2 thesis-fail | Tested 6 rule variants — **REFUTED**, all degraded PF |

**Root cause (structural):** Long-ATM-weekly at per-minute entry bleeds theta regardless of signal. Every filter-side improvement is in-sample noise — the theta drag is structural, not solvable by better entry timing or regime gating.

**Recommended pivots (from [[project-rules-verdict]] + E8 OOS falsification):**
1. **Sell-side premium** — short straddle/strangle on chop/range days
2. **Coarser horizon** — 5-min or 15-min ML entry (less noise floor, less theta drag)
3. **Different instrument** — NIFTY (tighter spreads, less theta-bleed per trade)
4. **E7 OOS** (pending, low confidence) — if it passes, one more window of long-ATM data to study before pivot

### OOS (Aug–Oct 2024, run_id `09298852`)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Trades | 44 |
| Win rate | **30%** |
| Net PF | **0.54** |
| Bootstrap CI | **[0.12, 1.36]** — fails gate on PF and lower bound |
| Cap PnL net | **−9.1%** |
| STOP_LOSS | **9 trades @ avg −24.5%** (vs 1 in-sample) |

### Why this is conclusive

1. **Magnitude of collapse** — PF 3.00 → 0.54 is not regression to a real mean of ~1.5; it is evidence of zero OOS edge.
2. **STOP_LOSS bucket 9×** — from 1 to 9 trades, all losing avg −24.5%. The engine gets trapped on the wrong side repeatedly; the regime tag identifies in-sample-favorable noise, not real regime structure.
3. **Tagger selection bias** — 5 taggers were tested on the same May–Jul window and the best was picked. Multiple-comparison inflation guaranteed the winning pattern wouldn't replicate.
4. **Pre-registered gate** — decision rule committed before OOS run. Result fails on both PF and CI lower bound. No retconning.

### What NOT to do (settled)

- Do NOT propose more regime tagger variants (gap thresholds, PCR cutoffs, combined-majority weights)
- Do NOT propose more entry-time-window combinations on the same dataset
- Do NOT iterate E8 with different filter combinations on May–Jul (same window → inflates apparent fit)
- Do NOT re-test E8 on a new window hoping for different result

Two env-driven entry gates, both off by default:

```python
is_in_configured_time_window(snap)        # reads ENTRY_TIME_WINDOWS="HH:MM-HH:MM,..."
compute_regime_tag(tagger, snap)          # 5 taggers: gap_03pct, open_vs_prev_02pct,
```

Gates are checked at the top of `_process_entry_votes` in `deterministic_rule_engine.py`. Regime tag is computed once per session (lazily, after ORB resolves).

| `ML_ENTRY_BLOCK_PE` | `0` | Block PE entries (CE-only) |
